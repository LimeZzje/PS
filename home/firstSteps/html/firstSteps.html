I've been trying for hours/days/centuries and can't get it. Help!

Pen spinning takes a lot of practice and is not something you will pick up quickly, unfortunately. If you are having trouble, especially with basic tricks, watch different tutorials, watch slomo's (YouTube has a native 0.5x and 0.25x slowmo function), and try to really understand why the trick works the way it does. Guide the pen through the motion with your other hand to help with the muscle memory. Keep practicing! If you really feel stuck ask for help, but be sure to include a video of you trying the trick. Saying "I can't do the Sonic please help" is the same as telling a stranger "I can't run fast please help." - Letho72[2019]

I don’t think my fingers are flexible enough to do that…

Everybody can learn penspinning. You don‘t need special fingers to be an elite spinner. The vast majority of tricks only need little movement of your fingers, so no worries, you don't need to fold prezels with your fingers. Even though, japanese spinner Fire@Fox does exactly that...

Notation

As you can see in the picture, every finger on your hand has a notation. The thumb is T (for thumb), index is 1, middle is 2, ring is 3 and pinky is 4. If you hold your pen between middle- and indexfinger, this position would be referred to as "12". The same goes for every other combination of pen positioning in your hand. Don't forget about weird ones like "14" where you would hold your pen with your index and pinkyfinger. If you would hold your pen with your thumb and index finger, this would be called "t1". I think you get the point. But why is this important? Every trick has a starting and an ending position. If I´d just say: "Do a 'Sonic'!" (Trickname) it would be unclear to you from which position to start. Because a regular "Sonic" can be done from: "34-23" and from "23-12". The precise way of saying it would be: "Do a Sonic 23-12". So you would know I want to see a Sonic starting from "23" (between ring and middlefinger) and ending in "12" (between middle and indexfinger).
